# Diet — Taking Control of What You Eat

**Diet** is a personal nutrition tracker I built to answer one simple question: *what am I actually putting into my body?*

Most diet apps are either over-engineered subscription traps or too rigid to fit real eating habits. I wanted something lightweight that I'd actually use.

## Core Features

### 🍎 Meal Logging

Log meals quickly with a minimal interface. Each entry captures:
- Food name and portion
- Calories, protein, carbs, and fat
- Meal time (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack)

### 📊 Daily Macro Dashboard

A clean summary view shows your daily totals against your targets — at a glance, not buried in menus.

### 🎯 Goal Setting

Set your own calorie and macro targets. The app calculates progress and gives you a clear picture of where you stand.

### 📅 History View

Browse past days to spot patterns — the kind of insight that actually changes behavior.

## Why I Built This

I've tried a dozen nutrition apps. They're either too complex (I don't need a social feed in my calorie counter) or not customizable enough. Building my own meant I could make it exactly as simple or detailed as I needed.

## What I Learned

1. **Nutrition data is messy** — even standardized values vary between sources, so rounding and sensible defaults matter
2. **Daily streaks and progress bars** are a surprisingly powerful motivator to keep logging
3. **Mobile-first layout** is non-negotiable for a tool you open right before or after meals
